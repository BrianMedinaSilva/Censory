# COMBAT 
This section of the strategy guide is dedicated to helping you better understand the unique combat system of *Censory*.

## SECTION 1 - PLAYER TURN
The player turn is when all strategic decisions are made on the player's end.

### 1.1 - SKILLS
You can type a number from 1 to 4 to use one of your currently equipped skills.

### 1.2 - TOGGLES
You can type the number 5 to activate or deactivate the current party's member toggle, which will change how your skills behave. This will not end your turn.

### 1.3 - ANALYZE
You can type the number 6 to see an teammate or enemy's stats in greater detail. This will not end your turn.

## SECTION 2 - ENEMY TURN
The enemy turn is when combat becomes more action-oriented.

### 2.1 - TIMED GUARDS
Whenever an enemy attempts to attack or debuff a party member, you must press ENTER as fast as possible in order to mitigate the effects.

Effects will be multiplied by the number of seconds it takes to press ENTER. This means if you take 0.5 seconds to react, you will suffer only half of the enemy skill's effects. It should be noted that the theoretical fastest human reaction time is approximately 1/10 of a second, meaning you can realistically reduce an enemy skill's effects by a maximum of 90%.

However, if an enemy targets another enemy, such as with a buff, no timing is performed and the full effect is always applied.

## SECTION 3 - END OF TURN
At the end of every turn, Health Velocity (HV) and Health Acceleration (HA) will be applied to all combatants, friend or foe, in the following manner:
1. If a combatant's HV is positive, they will restore HP. If their HV is negative, they will take damage.
2. If a combatant's HA is positive, their HV will rise. If their HA is negative, their HV will fall.

Think of it like Calculus:
- Health Position (HP): The current amount of HP a combatant has.
- Health Velocity (HV): The rate at which a combatant's HP changes every turn.
- Health Acceleration (HA): The rate at which a combatant's HV changes every turn.

## SECTION 4 - GRADING SYSTEM
After every battle, the party's performance shall be measured with a letter grade (A, B, C, D, or F). The grade earned is based on the sum of remaining HP across all party members divided by the sum of their maximum HP.

- If HP >= 0.9: A
- If HP >= 0.8: B
- If HP >= 0.7: C
- If HP >= 0.6: D
- Anything worse: F

You will not be penalized for poor grades, however certain rewards, such as new skills, will be locked behind earning a certain amount of As.
